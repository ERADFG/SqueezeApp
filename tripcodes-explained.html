Online Privacy Basics for Anonymous Posting

Using an anonymous platform is one layer of privacy. What you type, and how you browse, matters just as much. A few habits go a long way.

Watch what you write, not just where you post it

The most common way anonymous posts get traced back to a real identity isn't a technical leak — it's the content itself. Specific dates, locations, job titles, or writing style can identify someone even without a name attached. Before posting something sensitive, reread it as a stranger would.

Strip metadata from images

Photos can carry embedded metadata, including GPS coordinates and device information. If you're posting an image anywhere online, strip that metadata first using your OS's built-in tools or a dedicated app.

Be mindful of cross-posting

Posting the same message, in the same wording, across an anonymous board and a platform tied to your real identity can link the two together. If anonymity matters to you, write fresh text for anonymous posts rather than copy-pasting.

Consider your network-level privacy

An anonymous board doesn't hide your IP address from your internet provider or from the site's own abuse-prevention logs. If network-level privacy matters for your situation, a reputable VPN or Tor can add a layer the platform itself doesn't provide.

Read the privacy policy

Every platform handles logs differently. Check InteractInk's own Privacy Policy to see exactly what's collected and for how long.
